Fundación Macurayú

Arte que transforma, conecta y dignifica.

Arte que transforma, conecta y dignifica.

Somos una organización sin ánimo de lucro que promueve el desarrollo cognitivo, emocional y social de niños en situación de vulnerabilidad, especialmente aquellos sin acceso a diagnóstico o acompañamiento especializado.

Usamos el arte como herramienta de inclusión y desarrollo temprano, brindando talleres diseñados para estimular la creatividad, fortalecer la autoestima y facilitar procesos de autorregulación emocional en niños que, por su contexto, no han sido identificados ni atendidos por sistemas formales de salud o educación.

A diferencia de los programas dirigidos a poblaciones ya diagnosticadas, nuestros procesos están enfocados en la niñez invisible: aquella que, por falta de recursos u oportunidades, no accede a apoyos adecuados. En Macurayú, el arte no es un complemento, es un punto de partida.

Creemos en el arte como un lenguaje universal que protege, conecta y dignifica. Nuestra misión es abrir caminos de desarrollo para quienes más lo necesitan, desde la expresión artística libre, accesible y transformadora.

About us

The Macurayú Foundation is a nonprofit platform created to transform small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) into engines of formal employment, sustainable growth, and local development. We focus on businesses facing stagnation, contraction, or closure due to weak operational management, structural limitations, or lack of long-term vision. Instead of financial aid, we deliver hands-on, high-level guidance to strengthen core capabilities, align strategic direction, and enable sustainable scaling.

Our work, led by senior professionals, uses proven methodologies like Lean Management, Business Intelligence, Strategic Planning, and Six Sigma to drive real-time transformation in sectors with high employment potential in Colombia: agroindustry, processed foods, metalworking, artisanal mining, and light manufacturing.

Misión

Brindar acceso a procesos artísticos significativos a niños en situación de vulnerabilidad, usando el arte como herramienta de desarrollo cognitivo, emocional y social, especialmente en contextos donde no existen diagnósticos ni acompañamiento especializado.

Visión

Ser un referente en educación artística inclusiva en Colombia, reconocida por ofrecer alternativas reales de desarrollo y transformación a través del arte, para niños que han sido históricamente excluidos de los sistemas de apoyo tradicionales.

Líneas de acción

Our five-step intervention model includes:

1. Talleres de arte para la infancia vulnerable

Procesos pedagógicos diseñados para estimular la creatividad, la autorregulación y el pensamiento simbólico en niños sin diagnóstico ni atención especializada.

2. Formación de formadores

Capacitación a docentes, cuidadores y líderes comunitarios en el uso del arte como herramienta de inclusión, contención emocional y desarrollo infantil.

3. Intervención en territorio

Programas itinerantes en comunidades con altos índices de exclusión, llevando el arte a zonas rurales y urbano-marginales.

4. Alianzas intersectoriales

Trabajo conjunto con instituciones educativas, culturales y sociales para ampliar el alcance de nuestras acciones y garantizar sostenibilidad.

5. Investigación y documentación

Sistematización de experiencias, evaluación de impacto y generación de conocimiento sobre el rol del arte en contextos de vulnerabilidad infantil.

We work side-by-side with business leaders to build what we design, delivering strategic clarity and operational discipline without financial aid.

Staff & Governance.


The Macurayú Foundation maintains a lean, results-focused structure designed for agility, technical depth, and regional reach:

  • Executive Director: Provides strategic leadership, oversight, and partner relations.
  • Financial Director: Oversees financial strategy and compliance.
  • Operating Director: Manages operational execution and project delivery.
  • Strategic Director: Guides long-term vision and strategic planning.
  • Senior Sector Consultants: C-level professionals with expertise in agroindustry, processed foods, metalworking, artisanal mining, and manufacturing.
  • Junior Consultants: Project-based staff with sector expertise and field execution capabilities.
  • Administrative and Accounting Support: Outsourced for efficiency, transparency, and third-party validation.

This structure ensures tailored interventions with centralized quality control, ethical oversight, and cost efficiency.

Funding & Transparency

The Macurayú Foundation operates as a fully independent nonprofit (ESAL) in Colombia, funded exclusively by international NGOs, cooperation agencies, and multilateral institutions focused on inclusive economic development and formal job creation in Latin America. We do not receive public funds, transfer money to companies, or charge fees. Our funding comes from:

Cooperation

Strategic cooperation agreements with international foundations and NGOs.

Grants

Competitive international grants awarded for technical merit.

Development

Calls for proposals from multilateral institutions focused on economic resilience and private-sector development.

All resources are invested in technical execution to support SME transformation. Beneficiary SMEs receive high-level strategic direction and hands-on support at no cost, ensuring transparency and independence.

Our Work

What We Do

We support SMEs in key productive sectors—agroindustry, processed foods, artisanal mining, metalworking, and light manufacturing—to prevent collapse and unlock sustainable growth. Our rigorous, hands-on transformation model is tailored to SMEs with high employment potential. Implemented by senior executives using Lean Management, Business Intelligence, Strategic Planning, and Six Sigma, our approach focuses on execution within the company’s real environment. We rebuild strategy, strengthen operations, and guide execution to restore viability and unlock growth, ensuring SMEs become engines of sustainable, formal employment.

Priority Sectors


    We concentrate on sectors with high employment potential in Colombia:

  • Agroindustry: Enhancing value chains for sustainable agricultural production.
  • Processed Foods: Improving efficiency and market access for food processing SMEs.
  • Metalworking: Strengthening operational capacity for industrial growth.
  • Artisanal Mining: Promoting formalization and sustainable practices.
  • Light Manufacturing: Driving scalability and job creation in underserved regions.

These sectors are critical for creating decent, formal jobs and reactivating local economies.

Reports & Key Indicators


We track every intervention using a performance monitoring framework aligned with international cooperation standards. Key indicators include:

  • Formal Employment Created: Verified via official systems (PILA, RUAF, payroll).
  • EBITDA Growth:Measured through audited financials or tax filings.

We report using the ABCD model:

  • Achievements: Concrete results delivered.
  • Benefits: Economic and social value generated.
  • Concerns: Risks or operational constraints identified.
  • DoNext: Immediate next steps and priorities.
